EBWiki


I am one of the founders of EBWiki. In discussions with other members of the "Blacks in Technology" group, a global platform for black women and men in technology, we came up with the idea of keeping track of every case of black people killed by police, because in mainstream culture, we have observed that many people perceive that police don't kill black people as often as they do.

I helped build the initial technology platform and have been working with my teammates since 2015 to continuously gather and update the data so that it can be useful in ending the violence against people of color.

QueerGlobal


I help QueerGlobal, one of the volunteer projects I'm involved with, make decisions on how to proceed. I also help set up development practices for the tech department as well as mentor people working on individual projects and tasks. Finally, I'm helping to develop a roadmap and to define the value the tech department brings to QueerGlobal.

The Lamp


I served on the board of this nonprofit that was dedicated to providing training to help people be better at evaluating media to not be taken in by misinformation. This nonprofit had programs in several grade schools in the NYC area. We made recommendations for the program budget as well as set the direction of how the non-profit would expand.
